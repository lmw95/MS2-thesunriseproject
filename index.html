Take a look around...

700,000 years of history

From flint tools found near Kessingland, the home to the infamous Brittonic Iceni tribe, a rich maritime and naval history and a flourishing fishing trade during the 19th century.

A forgotten corner

Up until Sir Samuel Peto connected the coast to London by train in the early 1800s, large areas of the coast were untouched by the industrious activity of the 19th century.

This is East Anglia!

We are right on the eastern tip

The Sunrise Coast is home to Ness Point, Britain's most easterly point. Despite being by the sea, the area is in-fact on of the driest in the country, only recieving 787mm of rain each year!

A perfect location for your staycation

With two large towns, pockets of rugged countryside, access to the Broads and the sea, the Sunrise Coast is a complete package for those looking for adventure and a great family holiday to remember!

(Dont forget to follow the The Countryside Code)

A haven for wildlife

Thanks to the Broads and its marshy landscape, the Sunrise coast provides a home for many endangered species, including:

Swallowtail Butterfly

Water vole

Hi! I'm Rose.

I am on a one woman mission to show the world what the Sunrise Coast has to offer. Since COVID-19, the number of staycations in the UK has increased, and with more visitors than ever before, it seemed only fitting to produce a guide to welcome guests to the area!

That's where the Sunrise Project was born. Feel free to use and share this website to anyone coming to stay on the Sunrise Coast - by doing so you are supporting a small local business. I hope you enjoy your stay here!
