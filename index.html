The UK's most easterly coastline

The Sunrise Coast is the UK's most easterly coastline. It stretches from Great Yarmouth, all the way down to Kessingland, encompassing nature reserves, nationally recognised beaches, historic locations and the UK's most easterly point. It is called the Sunrise Coast as it is the first place in the UK to see the sunrise.

Did you know?

On Summer Solstice, the sun rises over Lowestoft at 4:30am.

More than 700,000 years of history

There is evidence of human activity along the Sunrise Coast for over 700,000 years! From flint tools found near Kessingland, the home to the infamous Brittonic Iceni tribe, a rich maritime and naval history and a flourishing fishing trade during the 19th century, the Sunrise Coast has seen the ages of humanity come and go.

A forgotten corner

Up until Sir Samuel Peto connected the coast to London by train in the early 1800s, large areas of the coast were untouched by the industrious activity of the 19th century, preserving a rugged rural & coastal landscape that later attracted conservationists and scientific interest to its pockets of undisturbed wildlife.

Did you know?

The UK's largest butterfly species only exists on The Norfolk Broads.
